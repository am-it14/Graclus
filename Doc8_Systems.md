Ohm’s Law is the fundamental circuitry rule dictating that the electrical current flowing through a conductor between two points is strictly and directly proportional to the voltage applied across those exact two points. It is absolutely crucial for calculating baseline parameters when designing circuits, summarized by $V = IR$. However, it is limited strictly to Ohmic materials. It reliably fails under extreme temperature variations or when applied to non-linear semiconductor devices like modern diodes and transistors.
